Conjunctions

Convention/Grammar

Conjunctions: Words that connect parts of a sentence, linking words, phrases, or clauses.

Coordinating conjunctions: Connect words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance in a sentence (remember FANBOYS: for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so).

Subordinating conjunctions: Introduce a dependent clause and connect it to an independent clause, showing relationships like time, cause, condition, or contrast.

Correlative conjunctions: Pairs of words that work together to connect equal elements in a sentence (e.g., both...and, either...or, neither...nor).


Words that I learnt from the story

Extravagant: Very expensive, elaborate, or showy; exceeding what is reasonable or necessary.
Sentence: The princess had an extravagant birthday party with a chocolate fountain and fireworks.

Accommodations: A place to stay or live, typically referring to rooms, lodging, or other living spaces.
Sentence: The hotel offers comfortable accommodations for all its guests.

Demonstrating: Showing or proving how something works or how to do something.
Sentence: The science teacher was demonstrating how to experiment safely.
